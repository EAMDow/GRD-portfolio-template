Behold The Designer

From the Ashes of a Broken home. To the Foster home where my spirit and self confidence was destroyed. I have risen from the flames and become a prime example of what a foster kid breaking the cycle. I have been making art since I was a small child, but I slowed down when I was put into foster care 19 years ago. Sometimes I even went years without making art, sinking into myself into what can only be described as a depression. During this time I was convinced that I should become a Vet. It was also during this time that my grandfather passed away which resulted in me drowing more in my own mind. I missed the entrance exam to get into the best schools for Vet Tech which I was using as a stepping stone to become a Vet. This resulted in me being accepted into Northern College which allowed me to get away from my Foster family and my blood family. The isolation allowed me to recover and find myself again, especially when COVID hit. In my final year of Vet Tech schooling I realized that being a Vet or a Vet Tech was not for me, and after talking with my Foster Care Case Worker, I decided to apply for the St.Clair College Graphic Design Program. It was in this program that I discovered my passion again, I found friends, and most importantly, I found my love for my work. I hope to take all of my expirience and make my mark in the feild of Graphic Design.
